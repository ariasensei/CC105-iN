🏛️ Data Governance Concepts

Definition & Purpose

Data Governance

A framework of decisions, processes, and systems that ensure the effective management, quality, security, and ethical use of organizational information assets. It establishes who has authority over data and how it can be used.

Governance Hierarchy

1

Strategic Level

Board & executive leadership set data vision, priorities, and risk appetite. Defines organizational data strategy.

2

Tactical Level

Data governance committees and stewards translate strategy into policies, standards, and procedures.

3

Operational Level

Data teams and custodians implement and enforce governance policies through daily data management activities.

⚙️ Key Governance Principles

👥 Accountability

Clear roles and responsibilities for data management with individuals accountable for data quality, security, and compliance.

🎯 Alignment

Data governance strategies align with business objectives and support organizational goals.

📊 Transparency

Clear communication of policies, decisions, and data use cases across the organization.

🔍 Standardization

Consistent policies, standards, and procedures for managing data across all systems and departments.

✅ Compliance

Adherence to legal, regulatory, and internal governance requirements.

🛡️ Risk Management

Proactive identification and mitigation of data-related risks.

📜 Key Information Policies

1 Data Classification Policy

Defines how data is categorized and handled based on sensitivity and value.

Classifications: Public, Internal, Confidential, Restricted

2 Data Access & Security Policy

Establishes who can access what data under what conditions with proper authentication and authorization.

Components: Role-based access, Multi-factor authentication, Encryption requirements

3 Data Retention & Disposal Policy

Specifies how long data is kept and how it should be securely deleted or archived.

Considerations: Legal requirements, Business needs, Secure deletion methods

4 Data Quality Policy

Establishes standards for accuracy, completeness, consistency, and timeliness of data.

Elements: Quality metrics, Validation rules, Issue resolution process

5 Data Sharing & Integration Policy

Controls how data can be shared internally/externally and integrated with other systems.

Governance: Approval workflows, Data agreements, Privacy considerations

6 Data Privacy & Compliance Policy

Ensures adherence to privacy regulations (GDPR, CCPA, etc.) and ethical data handling.

Requirements: Consent management, Privacy impact assessments, Breach notification

7 Data Breach & Incident Response Policy

Defines procedures for detecting, responding to, and recovering from data breaches.

Process: Detection, Containment, Investigation, Communication, Recovery

8 Audit & Monitoring Policy

Establishes requirements for logging, auditing, and monitoring data access and usage.

Activities: Log collection, Access reviews, Compliance audits, Anomaly detection

👨‍💼 Key Governance Roles

Chief Data Officer (CDO)

Executive-level leader responsible for establishing data strategy, governance framework, and organizational culture around data.

Data Owner

Senior manager accountable for specific data assets. Defines access rights, retention, and usage policies for their data.

Data Custodian

Technical team that manages data storage, backups, security, and infrastructure supporting data systems.

Data Steward

Subject matter experts who ensure data quality, consistency, and proper documentation of data definitions and business rules.

Data Governance Council

Cross-functional committee that makes decisions on data policies, prioritizes governance initiatives, and resolves conflicts.

Compliance Officer

Ensures adherence to legal and regulatory requirements, manages risk assessments, and oversees compliance monitoring.

👤 Individual Learning Activity

📋 Scenario: Regional Bank Data Governance Challenge

You are a newly appointed Data Governance Manager for TechBank, a regional financial institution with 500+ employees across 5 branches. The bank recently experienced a data breach affecting customer accounts and regulatory fines. Your task is to design a comprehensive data governance framework.

📌 Current Situation:

  • • No formal data governance structure exists
  • • Unclear data ownership and responsibility
  • • Inconsistent data quality across systems
  • • Limited access controls and monitoring
  • • Recent regulatory compliance issues

Task 1: Governance Structure (20 mins)

Design the governance hierarchy and key roles needed:

  • Define 5-6 key roles and their responsibilities
  • Create organizational reporting structure
  • Identify decision-making authority for each role

Task 2: Policy Framework (25 mins)

Develop 5 essential policies for TechBank:

  • Data classification policy with 4 levels
  • Access control policy with specific rules
  • Data retention and disposal guidelines
  • Data quality standards and metrics
  • Incident response procedures

Task 3: Risk Assessment (20 mins)

Identify and analyze governance risks:

  • List 5 key data governance risks facing the bank
  • Rate each risk by likelihood (High/Medium/Low)
  • Rate each risk by impact (High/Medium/Low)
  • Propose mitigation strategies for top 3 risks

Task 4: Implementation Roadmap (15 mins)

Create a phased implementation plan:

  • Phase 1 (Month 1-2): Quick wins and foundational setup
  • Phase 2 (Month 3-4): Policy implementation
  • Phase 3 (Month 5-6): Monitoring and optimization
  • Success metrics for each phase

Task 5: Compliance Alignment (15 mins)

Address regulatory requirements:

  • Identify applicable regulations (financial sector)
  • Map governance framework to compliance requirements
  • Propose audit and monitoring mechanisms
  • Create breach notification procedures

💡 Deliverables Summary:

Submit a comprehensive report including governance structure diagram, 5 detailed policies, risk assessment matrix, implementation roadmap with timelines, and compliance mapping. Total time: 90 minutes

👥 Group Learning Activity

🎯 Project: Healthcare Organization Data Governance

Group Size: 3-4 students | Duration: 120 minutes | Presentation: 15-20 minutes + 10 minutes Q&A

Your group works for MediCare Hospital System, which manages patient data across 3 hospitals and 15 clinics. With growing patient volume and increasing regulatory scrutiny, the organization needs a comprehensive data governance framework that addresses healthcare-specific compliance requirements.

🏥 Context - Healthcare Data Challenges:

  • • Patient health records (PHI) are highly sensitive and regulated (HIPAA)
  • • Multiple systems across hospitals with inconsistent data standards
  • • Need to balance data sharing for patient care with privacy
  • • Regulatory audits expected within 6 months
  • • Recent security incidents exposed patient privacy concerns

1 Governance Framework Design (30 minutes)

Develop comprehensive governance structure:

✓ Organizational Structure: Design governance committees, roles (CDO, data stewards for clinical/administrative data, privacy officer), and reporting hierarchy

✓ Data Classification: Create 5 data categories specific to healthcare (Public, Internal, Patient Data, Sensitive Clinical, Genetic Data)

✓ Governance Principles: List 6 principles aligned with patient care, compliance, and ethical data use

✓ Decision-Making Framework: Define how governance council makes decisions on data use requests

2 Policy Development (35 minutes)

Create 6 comprehensive healthcare policies:

✓ Patient Data Privacy Policy: HIPAA compliance, consent management, patient rights

✓ Clinical Data Quality Standards: Data accuracy requirements for patient safety

✓ Access Control Policy: Role-based access for clinicians, researchers, administrators

✓ Data Sharing for Research: Requirements for using patient data in research

✓ Breach Response Protocol: Procedures for HIPAA breach notification and investigation

✓ Data Retention Policy: How long to retain patient records per healthcare regulations

3 Compliance & Risk Management (35 minutes)

Address regulatory and risk requirements:

✓ Regulatory Mapping: Align governance framework with HIPAA, state privacy laws, and medical records regulations

✓ Risk Assessment: Identify 8+ data governance risks specific to healthcare (data breaches, patient safety, compliance violations, interoperability issues)

✓ Audit Plan: Create internal audit strategy for policy compliance

✓ Incident Response: Develop procedures for data breach investigation and regulatory reporting

✓ Monitoring Mechanisms: Define how to track governance effectiveness and data quality

4 Implementation Roadmap (20 minutes)

Create phased implementation strategy:

✓ Phase 1 (Month 1-2): Establish governance structure, identify quick wins

✓ Phase 2 (Month 3-4): Implement core policies and training programs

✓ Phase 3 (Month 5-6): Deploy monitoring and audit mechanisms

✓ Success Metrics: KPIs for each phase, regulatory readiness assessment

✓ Resource Requirements: Budget, personnel, technology needs

📊 Presentation Requirements:

  • ✓ Visual Aids: Governance org chart, policy matrix, risk assessment table, roadmap timeline
  • ✓ Key Points: Explain why each governance element is critical for healthcare
  • ✓ Challenges: Discuss implementation challenges and how your team would address them
  • ✓ Sustainability: How will governance evolve as the organization grows?
  • ✓ Q&A Preparation: Be ready to defend policy decisions and discuss trade-offs

🎓 Evaluation Criteria:

  • • Comprehensiveness of governance framework (30%)
  • • Healthcare-specific compliance understanding (25%)
  • • Feasibility of implementation plan (20%)
  • • Presentation clarity and teamwork (15%)
  • • Q&A responses and critical thinking (10%)
