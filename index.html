INFORMATION MANAGEMENT

Instructor: MKDB

Course Description

Information Management is a foundational course that introduces students to the concepts, principles, and practices involved in collecting, organizing, storing, processing, securing, and utilizing information effectively within organizations. The course focuses on how data is transformed into meaningful information to support decision-making, operational efficiency, and strategic planning. Students will explore information systems, databases, data lifecycle management, information security, data governance, and ethical considerations in managing information. Through lectures, discussions, case studies, and hands-on activities, learners will gain practical and analytical skills essential for managing information resources in modern, technology-driven environments.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of the semester you will be able to:

  • 1 Explain the fundamental concepts, importance, and role of information management in organizations.
  • 2 Describe the information lifecycle, including data collection, storage, processing, retrieval, and disposal.
  • 3 Apply basic principles of database and information systems management to real-world scenarios.
  • 4 Analyze information quality, security, privacy, and ethical issues related to information management.
  • 5 Propose effective information management solutions that support organizational decision-making and performance.
