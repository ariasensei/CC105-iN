🔐 Data Privacy Principles

🌍 GDPR

General Data Protection Regulation

  • ✓ Lawful basis for processing
  • ✓ Right to be forgotten
  • ✓ Data portability
  • ✓ Privacy by design
  • ✓ Data breach notification (72 hours)

Applies to: EU citizens and residents

🇺🇸 CCPA

California Consumer Privacy Act

  • ✓ Right to know what data is collected
  • ✓ Right to delete personal data
  • ✓ Right to opt-out of data sales
  • ✓ Right to non-discrimination
  • ✓ Consumer rights for minors (13-16)

Applies to: California residents

🍁 PIPEDA

Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act

  • ✓ Accountability principle
  • ✓ Identifying purposes clearly
  • ✓ Consent requirements
  • ✓ Limited data collection
  • ✓ Accuracy and security

Applies to: Canadian individuals

✂️ Data Minimization

Collect Only What You Need

  • ✓ Limit data to purpose
  • ✓ Reduce storage duration
  • ✓ Regular data audits
  • ✓ Anonymization techniques
  • ✓ Delete obsolete data

Best practice: Privacy by design

⚖️ Ethical Concerns in Information Management

👁️ Surveillance & Monitoring

Ethical Issue: Tracking user behavior without consent

Concern: Employees monitored excessively • Users tracked online • Location data collected • Behavioral profiling without awareness

🎯 Targeted Discrimination

Ethical Issue: Using data to discriminate or manipulate

Concern: Algorithmic bias • Predatory targeting • Pricing discrimination • Denial of services based on profiles

📊 Data Exploitation

Ethical Issue: Selling or misusing personal data

Concern: Third-party data sales • Hidden data monetization • Lack of transparency • Data brokers

🤖 Algorithmic Decision Making

Ethical Issue: Lack of transparency in AI decisions

Concern: Black-box algorithms • No explainability • Biased outcomes • Appeal rights missing

👶 Children's Data Protection

Ethical Issue: Inadequate protection for minors

Concern: Collection from children without parental consent • Educational data misuse • Long-term digital footprint

⚠️ Information Asymmetry

Ethical Issue: Users unaware of data practices

Concern: Complex privacy policies • Hidden data practices • Uninformed consent • Power imbalance

⚖️ Legal Framework & Compliance

📋 Key Legal Obligations

  • 1. Transparency: Disclose how data is collected and used
  • 2. Consent: Obtain explicit permission before collection
  • 3. Security: Protect data from unauthorized access
  • 4. Accountability: Maintain records of processing activities
  • 5. User Rights: Allow access, correction, and deletion requests
  • 6. Breach Notification: Report data breaches to authorities and affected individuals

⚠️ Penalties for Non-Compliance

GDPR: Up to €20M or 4% of annual revenue
CCPA: Up to $7,500 per violation
PIPEDA: Fines + court orders
HIPAA: Up to $1.5M per violation

📋 Administrative Controls

👤 Individual Activity (20 minutes)

📱 Scenario: Social Media Data Breach

Situation: A popular social media platform stores user data including names, emails, phone numbers, location, and browsing history. A security breach exposes the personal information of 50 million users. The company delayed notifying users for 3 weeks.

Answer the following questions:

  1. Identify violations: Which privacy regulations (GDPR, CCPA, PIPEDA) were violated? Explain why.
  2. Ethical concerns: What ethical issues are involved in this breach?
  3. Legal consequences: What penalties could the company face?
  4. Affected rights: What user rights were violated?
  5. Remediation: Propose 5 corrective actions the company should take immediately and long-term.

✏️ Deliverables:

  • • 2-3 page written analysis
  • • Identification of at least 3 specific legal violations
  • • List of immediate and long-term actions
  • • Reference to relevant regulations

👥 Group Activity (45 minutes)

🏥 Scenario: Healthcare Organization Privacy Framework

Situation: You are a privacy team at a regional hospital network collecting and managing patient health records (PHI). The organization needs to develop a comprehensive data privacy and ethics framework compliant with HIPAA, GDPR (for international patients), and industry best practices.

Group Deliverables (Divide work among team members):

  1. Privacy Policy & Legal Framework
    • • Draft key sections of privacy policy
    • • Map regulatory requirements (HIPAA, GDPR)
    • • Define consent mechanisms
    • • Outline breach notification procedures
  2. Ethical Guidelines
    • • Develop code of conduct for data handling
    • • Address ethical concerns in patient monitoring
    • • Define appropriate data use boundaries
    • • Create fairness principles for AI in diagnosis
  3. Technical & Administrative Controls
    • • Design encryption and access controls
    • • Create data retention schedule
    • • Develop incident response plan
    • • Define audit and monitoring procedures
  4. Implementation Roadmap
    • • Timeline for compliance
    • • Staff training schedule
    • • Compliance verification process
    • • Cost-benefit analysis

📊 Presentation Requirements:

  • • 15-20 minute presentation
  • • Visual slides with key frameworks
  • • Q&A session (5-10 minutes)
  • • Written report (5-8 pages)
  • • Appendix with sample documents
