01  What is Business Intelligence?

Business Intelligence (BI) is the process of collecting, analyzing, and interpreting data to support business decision-making. It transforms raw facts into actionable insights that help organizations understand what's happening, why it's happening, and what to do next.

🎓 Real-World Example: A college canteen manager notices Tuesday sales are slow. Without BI, they'd guess. With BI, they check data: Tuesday evening classes are fewer. Action: Offer 3-5pm happy hour discount. Result: Tuesday sales jump 40%, and now they know which promo works.

BI helps leaders answer critical questions: "Why did revenue drop?" "Which customers are loyal?" "Should we expand?" "What's overstocked?" Without data, these decisions are just gambles.

03  Key BI Concepts

📌

KPI — Key Performance Indicator

A measurable value showing how well something performs.

Examples: Revenue, customer satisfaction, website traffic, employee retention.

📊

Metric

A quantifiable measurement to track progress.

Examples: Average order value, conversion rate, cart abandonment, bounce rate.

📺

Dashboard

A visual display of metrics and KPIs, real-time updated.

One-page view showing key data via charts, gauges, and alerts.

📄

Report

A detailed summary with historical trends and analysis.

Deep-dive view showing comparisons, forecasts, and explanations.

1

06  Case Study: Retail Business BI in Action

🏪 The Scenario: FastMart Regional Convenience Stores

THE PROBLEM

FastMart operates 45 stores across Metro Manila. Profit margins are dropping. Some stores are thriving, others struggling. Management doesn't know why or what to do.

THE QUESTIONS

Why are margins down? Which stores underperform? What products should we stock more? Where should we open next?

What BI Did:

  1. 1. Collected Data — Point-of-sale systems from all 45 stores: sales, inventory, customer counts, foot traffic.
  2. 2. Analyzed Patterns — Found: Store #12 (Makati) does ₱850K/month, Store #38 (Caloocan) does ₱320K. Why?
  3. 3. Drilled Deeper — Traffic similar. But Store #12 customers buy snacks+beverage combo (₱280 avg). Store #38 mostly snacks (₱95 avg).
  4. 4. Discovered Root Cause — Store #12 has coolers near checkout. Store #38 doesn't. Small difference, huge impact.
  5. 5. Created Dashboard — Real-time view: sales by store, product mix, profit margin by location, customer behavior.

🎯 Decision & Action Taken

DECISION 1 (Operational)

What: Install coolers near checkout in all 45 stores. Cost: ₱85K/store. Expected: Beverage sales ↑ 22%, margin ↑ 4%. Timeline: 3 months.

DECISION 2 (Tactical)

What: Stock premium beverages in high-traffic stores (#12, #5, #8). Generic brands in others. Expected: Reduce dead inventory 18%, improve margin. Timeline: Monthly review.

DECISION 3 (Strategic)

What: Next store location: Open in high-foot-traffic areas (BGC, Makati Central) with cooler+premium mix model. Avoid low-traffic suburbs. Timeline: 2024-2025.

💬 Class Discussion: What Would You Do?

Question 1: Diagnosis

Store #38 has 18% beverage sales vs Store #12's 34%. What other data would you request to confirm the cooler placement hypothesis?

Hint: Think about foot traffic patterns, location, customer demographics, store layout, competitor proximity.

Question 2: Risk

FastMart decides to install coolers. But Store #38 is in a low-traffic area with limited foot traffic. Could adding coolers hurt or fail? Why or why not?

Hint: Consider capacity utilization, storage costs, spoilage, customer base size.

Question 3: Strategy

The CEO wants to "close underperforming stores" to cut costs. Using BI, how would you evaluate this decision? Should all low-margin stores close?

Hint: Consider location value, market position, brand presence, future growth potential, employee impact.

Question 4: Metrics

FastMart tracks 45 store metrics monthly. Which 3-5 KPIs should appear on the executive dashboard (1-page view for CEO)? Why those?

Hint: Think about what a CEO needs to know to make strategic decisions in 5 minutes.

07  Real-World BI Success Stories

🛒 E-Commerce (Shopee/Lazada)

Insight: Cart abandonment rate 45% when shipping ₱150+ (>15% of order). Action: Free shipping on orders >₱500. Result: Completion ↑28%, revenue ↑35%.

🏥 Hospital

Insight: ER wait times peak 2-4pm with only 2 doctors. Action: Schedule 3 doctors during peak hours. Result: Wait times ↓ from 90min to 30min, patient satisfaction ↑.

📱 Social Media (TikTok)

Insight: 15-30sec vertical videos get 3x engagement vs longer. Action: Promote short-form in algorithm. Result: Session time ↑25%, ad revenue ↑40%.

🎓 University

Insight: CS grads have 92% job placement; Liberal Arts 64%. Action: Market CS programs, revise LA curriculum. Result: CS enrollment ↑18%.

08  Your Turn: Reflection Activity

📋 Guided Worksheet: Apply BI to Your Own Context

Choose a business, organization, or scenario you know (your part-time job, school, family business, social media, etc.). Apply the BI mindset.

Step 1: Identify the Organization

What business or organization do you want to analyze?

Include: Type (retail, food, tech, etc.) and location/scope.

Step 2: The Problem

What challenge or question does this org face?

Avoid: "We want more sales." Be specific about the problem.

Step 3: What Data Would You Collect?

List 3-5 data points to diagnose this problem.

Think: What numbers would help you understand the problem?

Step 4: Hypothesized Insight

What do you predict the data will show? (Your guess before analyzing.)

This is your educated guess. No wrong answer.

Step 5: Decision You'd Make

If your hypothesis is correct, what action would you take?

At what level? Operational (daily)? Tactical (monthly)? Strategic (annual)?

Step 6: Measure Success

What KPI would you track to measure if the decision worked?

How would you know if your action was successful?
