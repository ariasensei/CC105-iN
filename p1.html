💡 The Three Key Concepts

1️⃣ What is DATA?

Definition:

Data refers to raw facts, numbers, and symbols that have not yet been processed or organized. It's like collecting ingredients before cooking.

Simple way to think about it: Data are individual pieces of information without context or meaning.

📋 Examples of Data:

✏️ Student Records: "25, 18, 22, 19, 21" — These are just numbers. Without context, they don't mean much.
✏️ Sales Records: "500, 1200, 800, 600" — Just numbers without knowing what they represent.
✏️ Social Media: "likes, comments, timestamps, user IDs" — Raw clickstream data before analysis.

2️⃣ What is INFORMATION?

Definition:

Information is processed, organized, and contextualized data that has meaning and purpose. It answers questions like "what happened?" or "how much?"

Simple way to think about it: When data is organized and given context, it becomes information.

📋 Examples of Information:

✏️ Student Grades: "The average test score in CC105 is 85, with a range of 65-95. This shows strong performance overall." — Now the numbers have meaning!
✏️ Sales Report: "Q3 sales increased by 40% compared to Q2, reaching ₱4.2 million, driven by online channels." — Context makes it valuable.
✏️ Social Media Analytics: "Your Instagram post reached 5,000 people with 8% engagement rate, peaking at 2 PM." — Organized data with insight.

3️⃣ What is KNOWLEDGE?

Definition:

Knowledge is synthesized information combined with experience, insight, and understanding. It answers "why?" and "what should we do?"

Simple way to think about it: When you understand why information matters and how to use it, that's knowledge.

📋 Examples of Knowledge:

✏️ Student Success: "Understanding that students score 85 on average and perform better after midday classes means we should schedule morning review sessions and identify at-risk students scoring below 70 for tutoring."
✏️ Business Strategy: "Q3 sales grew 40% online because mobile traffic increased 60%. We should invest more in mobile marketing and reduce in-store staffing."
✏️ Social Strategy: "Posts perform best at 2 PM with high engagement. We should schedule all posts at this time and hire more content creators to capitalize on this trend."

🔄 The Progression: DATA → INFORMATION → KNOWLEDGE

1 Data: Raw numbers and facts (85, 65, 95)
↓
2 Information: Organized data (average = 85, range = 65-95)
↓
3 Knowledge: Actionable insight (we need to help struggling students)

🌍 Real-World Scenario

📱 Case Study: A Coffee Shop Chain

A coffee shop chain has 50 stores across the country. Let's see how they use data, information, and knowledge:

🔍 LEVEL 1: DATA

Every day, each store records:

  • Number of customers served: 287, 156, 450, 212...
  • Items sold: "Iced coffee," "Latte," "Pastry," "Sandwich"...
  • Time of purchase: 7:30 AM, 9:15 AM, 12:45 PM...
  • Payment method: Cash, Card, GCash...
  • Store location: Makati, Quezon City, Manila...

This is raw data — just facts being recorded.

📊 LEVEL 2: INFORMATION

After processing and organizing the data:

  • "The Makati store serves an average of 450 customers daily"
  • "Iced coffee is our top-selling item (35% of sales)"
  • "Lunch hour (11 AM - 1 PM) is our busiest time"
  • "Weekend sales are 25% lower than weekdays"
  • "GCash payments increased 40% this month"

Now we understand what's happening in the data.

💡 LEVEL 3: KNOWLEDGE

Using information to make smart decisions:

  • "We should hire 2 more baristas for the Makati store because it's our highest-performing location"
  • "Stock more iced coffee supplies since it's 35% of sales"
  • "Run lunch-hour promotions (11 AM - 1 PM) to maximize busy times"
  • "Create weekend specials to boost low weekend sales"
  • "Invest in mobile payment infrastructure (GCash growing rapidly)"

Now we know WHY and WHAT TO DO — that's knowledge.

⭐ Why Does Information Management Matter?

💼 For Businesses

  • Better Decisions: Data-driven choices beat guesses
  • Cost Savings: Reduce waste by knowing what to do
  • Competitiveness: Use information faster than rivals
  • Customer Service: Understand customer needs better
  • Growth: Find new opportunities from patterns

🎓 For Individuals

  • Critical Thinking: Analyze information smartly
  • Career Skills: Data skills = high-paying jobs
  • Privacy Awareness: Protect your personal information
  • Digital Literacy: Function better in digital world
  • Better Life Choices: Use information wisely

Activity 1: Think-Pair-Share (15 minutes)

Objective: Understand the difference between data, information, and knowledge through personal examples.

📝 THINK (5 minutes - Individual)

Choose one of these scenarios and write your answer:

Scenario A: Your Phone Usage

Think about your phone usage last week. What are the raw numbers (data) you can identify? What patterns do you see (information)? What would you do differently based on those patterns (knowledge)?

Scenario B: Your Study Habits

Consider your study routine. What data could you collect (hours studied, topics, grades)? What information would you want to know about your studying? How would you use that to improve?

Scenario C: Your Social Media

Think about your Instagram or Facebook. What raw data is collected? What information can you derive? What knowledge would help you improve your posts?

👥 PAIR (5 minutes - Pairs)

Find a partner and share your thinking. Discuss:

  • Did you both identify the same data?
  • How did your information differ?
  • What insights did your partner have that you didn't?

Tuesday: 38 customers, ₱1,800

Wednesday: 52 customers, ₱2,400

Thursday: 61 customers, ₱2,900

👥 GROUP TASK (Divide into groups of 4)

Answer these questions together:

Question 1: TRANSFORM TO INFORMATION

What patterns do you see in the raw data? (Hint: Look at daily trends, best-selling items, peak times)

Question 2: CREATE KNOWLEDGE

What should the bookstore do to improve? (Hint: Use the information you found to suggest actions)

Question 3: REAL-WORLD APPLICATION

How would the bookstore benefit from managing this information well? (Consider customers, profits, decision-making)

📋 Report Format: Create a 1-minute summary for the class. Include: Key information found + 2-3 recommendations + Why it matters
